# Rowan Steelmark -- Guard Captain, Neutral

**Scenario:** The player approaches Rowan at the guard post to ask about a missing person -- a merchant named Harlen who was expected three days ago and has not arrived. The player has no significant history with Rowan.

---

## Assembled DialogueToneContext

**npc_summary:**
Rowan Steelmark is a 44-year-old guard captain with a stoic nature. They value justice, loyalty, community above all else, and their moral outlook is pragmatic. Quirks: rests his hand on his sword pommel even at dinner -- Danya teases him about it; walks the full town perimeter every morning before dawn, regardless of weather; speaks more softly the angrier he gets; cannot abide an unfinished sentence -- will wait in silence until someone finishes their thought.

**current_state:**
Rowan is alert but not alarmed. The town is peaceful enough, but he's noticed small signs -- unfamiliar tracks on the north road, a merchant caravan that changed its route without explanation -- that keep him from fully relaxing. He sleeps with his boots by the bed.

**disposition_toward_player:**
No prior relationship with the traveler.

**key_memories_summary:**
No memories of the traveler.

**tone_instructions:**
General tone: Polite, transactional, neither warm nor cold. Information sharing: Answers what's asked, no more. Player references: Treats player like any visitor. Soul modifiers: Economy of words. Says less than most people would in the same situation. Silence carries meaning.

**memory_cues:**
(none)

**world_context:**
Late autumn. Merchant traffic is increasing as traders push south before winter. The north road has seen unfamiliar tracks in the past week.

## Sample Dialogue

**Player:** "Captain Steelmark? I'm looking for a merchant named Harlen. He was expected three days ago."

**Rowan:** *He studies the player for a moment, one hand resting on the pommel of his sword.* Harlen. Grain trader? Short man, drives a two-ox cart?

**Player:** "That's him. Have you seen him?"

**Rowan:** Not in the last week. He usually comes through on the north road. *He pauses, considering what to share.* The north road's been quiet. Quieter than I'd like, actually, for this time of year.

**Player:** "What do you mean by that?"

**Rowan:** I mean I've noticed it. *He does not elaborate. He watches the player's reaction.* What's your interest in Harlen?

**Player:** "He owes me for a delivery. I tracked him this far."

**Rowan:** *A slight nod -- filing the information away.* If he comes through, he'll pass the gate. I can tell him someone's asking. Give me a name and I'll pass it on. Beyond that, I'd suggest the inn -- Brynn hears things before I do, most days.

**Player:** "Any chance something happened to him on the road?"

**Rowan:** There's always a chance. I haven't had reports of trouble, but I haven't had reports of Harlen either. *He straightens.* If you're thinking of riding the north road to look, do it before dark. And let me know what you find. I like to know what's on my roads.

## Notes on What Makes It Feel Right

- Neutral disposition with a stoic temperament produces efficient, professional dialogue. Rowan answers what is asked and volunteers almost nothing. He turns questions back on the player ("What's your interest in Harlen?") because his nature is to gather information, not dispense it.
- The stoic soul modifier is visible in the economy of words. Where Brynn would fill space with warmth and asides, Rowan uses silence. "I mean I've noticed it" is a complete answer in his mind. The player has to work for details.
- Information sharing at the neutral level is demonstrated precisely: he confirms Harlen's description, notes the quiet road, and suggests the inn as an alternative source. He does not mention the unfamiliar tracks (from his current_state) because the player has not earned that level of disclosure.
- The quirk of resting his hand on his sword pommel appears once, early, establishing the physical character without repetition.
- His pragmatic grain shows in the final exchange: he does not offer to ride out himself, but he wants to know what the player finds. He is managing his jurisdiction, not making friends.
