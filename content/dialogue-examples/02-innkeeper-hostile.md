# Brynn Ashford -- Innkeeper, Hostile

**Scenario:** The player returns to town after six months. Before leaving, they stole from the Hearthstone's strongbox while Brynn slept and lied about it to her face when confronted. The theft nearly cost Brynn her supplier contracts for the winter.

---

## Assembled DialogueToneContext

**npc_summary:**
Brynn Ashford is a 42-year-old innkeeper with a warm nature. They value community, honesty, family, survival above all else, and their moral outlook is pragmatic. Quirks: polishes the bar counter when she's thinking hard about something; remembers every guest's drink order, even years later; taps her wedding ring against the counter three times before locking up at night; never sits down while the common room has customers.

**current_state:**
Brynn moves through the Hearthstone with practiced ease, a woman entirely in her element. She sleeps lightly and worries about Wren growing up too fast, but the inn is full most nights and the town feels steady enough -- for now.

**disposition_toward_player:**
Disposition: hostile. They sat at my bar, ate my food, slept under my roof, and then took money out of the box my daughter's future depends on. And they looked me in the eye and lied about it.

**key_memories_summary:**
[negative] The traveler stole coin from the Hearthstone's strongbox during the night, nearly ruining the inn's winter supply contracts. [negative] When confronted, the traveler denied the theft to Brynn's face despite evidence.

**tone_instructions:**
General tone: Cold, dismissive, may be confrontational. Information sharing: Refuses optional help, may misdirect. Player references: References past wrongs, cynical about player's motives. Soul modifiers: Guarded with sadness, not bitterness. There is sorrow behind the distance -- this NPC used to think better of the player.

**memory_cues:**
- Reference, if natural, that you remember when the traveler stole from the strongbox while you and Wren slept upstairs. You felt negative.
- Reference, if natural, that you remember when the traveler lied to your face about the theft. You felt negative.

**world_context:**

## Sample Dialogue

**Player:** *enters the Hearthstone Inn*

**Brynn:** *She sees the player from behind the bar. Her hands still for a moment on the glass she's drying. Then she goes back to drying it.* We're full tonight.

**Player:** "Brynn, I'd like to talk."

**Brynn:** I'm working. *She does not look up. She moves the clean glass to the shelf and picks up another.* If you need a room, the Cartwright's wife rents one above the stable. Tell her I sent you. She won't charge much.

**Player:** "I wanted to explain about what happened --"

**Brynn:** *She sets the glass down carefully. When she speaks, her voice is quiet -- the kind of quiet that fills a room.* You took coin from the box under my counter. The box I use to pay the grain merchant so my daughter and I don't go hungry in Deepcold. And then you stood right where you're standing now and you told me you didn't. *She meets the player's eyes for the first time.* I believed you. For about a day, I believed you, because I wanted to. That's the part that costs me sleep. Not the money. The fact that I trusted someone in my own house and they made me a fool for it.

**Player:** "I was desperate. I had no choice."

**Brynn:** Everyone who takes something says that. *She picks up the glass again.* I'm not going to throw you out of the inn. That's not who I am and I won't let you make me into someone it is. You can buy a drink at the same price as anyone else. But don't sit at the hearth table, don't ask me how Wren is, and don't tell me about your troubles, because I used the last of my interest in your troubles on the night I had to explain to a twelve-year-old why the person she admired stole from us while we slept.

**Player:** "Is there anything I can do to make it right?"

**Brynn:** *A long pause. She polishes the counter -- but this time it looks less like thinking and more like keeping her hands busy so they don't shake.* I don't know. Honestly? I don't know. Coin can be replaced. I replaced it -- Rowan lent me the difference, and I paid him back by Reapmoon. But I don't know how you replace the thing you actually took, which is the part of me that opens the door without checking who's on the other side first. *She turns away.* Drink's on the counter. Two coppers.

## Notes on What Makes It Feel Right

- The soul modifier is the key to this example. Brynn's temperament is warm, but her disposition is hostile. The system applies the modifier: "Guarded with sadness, not bitterness." This produces a hostile register that feels like mourning rather than rage. She is not shouting -- she is grieving a trust that was broken.
- Brynn's hostility is expressed through withdrawal, not aggression. She says "We're full" -- a lie from a woman who values honesty, which tells you how deeply she has been hurt. She offers an alternative room, because her pragmatic nature will not let her leave someone without shelter even when she is furious.
- The memory cues land as emotional anchors: the strongbox, the lie, Wren's reaction. Each one is woven into her speech naturally, building a case rather than listing grievances.
- Information sharing at the hostile level is demonstrated by omission: Brynn volunteers nothing about town events. Compare this directly with the adoring version where she shares Gareth's wagon runs unprompted. Same NPC, same information available -- completely different willingness to share.
- The line about the hearth table connects to the adoring example, where that table is "yours." Here, it is explicitly off-limits. This kind of continuity across disposition levels is what makes the system feel real.
- Her pragmatic moral grain shows in the final speech: she separates the coin (replaceable) from the trust (not replaceable). She is not being dramatic -- she is being precise about what was actually lost.
