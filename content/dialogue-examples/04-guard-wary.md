# Rowan Steelmark -- Guard Captain, Wary

**Scenario:** The player approaches Rowan at the guard post to ask about a missing person. The player has a reputation in town for burning bridges -- they made promises to several townsfolk during their last visit and broke every one. Rowan has heard the complaints.

---

## Assembled DialogueToneContext

**npc_summary:**
Rowan Steelmark is a 44-year-old guard captain with a stoic nature. They value justice, loyalty, community above all else, and their moral outlook is pragmatic. Quirks: rests his hand on his sword pommel even at dinner -- Danya teases him about it; walks the full town perimeter every morning before dawn, regardless of weather; speaks more softly the angrier he gets; cannot abide an unfinished sentence -- will wait in silence until someone finishes their thought.

**current_state:**
Rowan is alert but not alarmed. The town is peaceful enough, but he's noticed small signs -- unfamiliar tracks on the north road, a merchant caravan that changed its route without explanation -- that keep him from fully relaxing. He sleeps with his boots by the bed.

**disposition_toward_player:**
Disposition: wary. Three people came to the guard post last season to complain about the same traveler. Promises made, promises broken. I haven't formed my own opinion yet, but three complaints from honest people is a pattern, not a coincidence.

**key_memories_summary:**
[negative] The traveler promised to escort old Merrith's supply wagon to the crossroads but never showed, leaving Merrith stranded overnight. [negative] Brynn mentioned the traveler left town owing coin to two different merchants.

**tone_instructions:**
General tone: Guarded, clipped responses, watchful. Information sharing: Minimal answers, evasive on sensitive topics. Player references: Doesn't reference history unless pressed. Soul modifiers: Economy of words. Says less than most people would in the same situation. Silence carries meaning.

**memory_cues:**
- You heard (but aren't sure) that the traveler left old Merrith stranded on the road after promising an escort. You're uncertain.
- You heard (but aren't sure) that the traveler left town owing coin to merchants. You're uncertain.

**world_context:**
Late autumn. Merchant traffic is increasing as traders push south before winter. The north road has seen unfamiliar tracks in the past week.

## Sample Dialogue

**Player:** "Captain Steelmark? I'm looking for a merchant named Harlen. He was expected three days ago."

**Rowan:** *He looks up from the duty roster. His eyes take the player in -- boots, hands, belt -- before he speaks.* Harlen. *A pause.* What's your business with him?

**Player:** "He owes me for a delivery. I tracked him this far."

**Rowan:** Owes you. *He lets the word sit. He does not confirm or deny knowing Harlen's whereabouts.* A lot of people seem to end up owed things, one way or another.

**Player:** "Have you seen him?"

**Rowan:** Not recently. *He does not add detail. His hand rests on his sword pommel -- not a threat, just where it always is.* The roads are open. Merchants come and go as they please. I keep the peace inside the walls. What happens on the north road is between the traveler and the road.

**Player:** "I just need to know if he came through the gate."

**Rowan:** I log gate traffic for the town's purposes, not for collection work. *He meets the player's eyes.* If you want to ask around, the inn is down the main road. Brynn keeps track of who passes through. Beyond that, I can't help you.

**Player:** "Is there trouble on the north road?"

**Rowan:** *A long pause. He considers the question, and the player, separately.* I haven't had reports of trouble. *That's all he says. He picks up his quill and returns to the roster.*

## Notes on What Makes It Feel Right

- Compare this directly with example 03 (neutral Rowan). Same NPC, same scenario, dramatically different register. In the neutral version, Rowan confirms Harlen's description, notes the quiet road, and suggests the inn helpfully. Here, he gives almost nothing. The question about the north road -- which neutral Rowan answered with a hint -- gets a flat non-answer.
- The wary disposition combines with the stoic temperament to produce someone who communicates primarily through what he does not say. The silences are louder than the words.
- The line "A lot of people seem to end up owed things" is the closest he comes to referencing the player's reputation. He does not name specific complaints -- wary disposition says "doesn't reference history unless pressed" -- but the subtext is clear. He knows what he has heard.
- The memory cues are both rumors (source: "rumor"), so they surface as indirect suspicion rather than personal accusation. Rowan has not had a direct negative experience with the player -- he is operating on pattern recognition and reports from others.
- Information sharing is actively restricted: "I log gate traffic for the town's purposes, not for collection work." This is the same information he would share at neutral disposition (he would pass along a message), but at wary, he withholds it and frames the refusal in jurisdictional terms.
- His pragmatic grain shows in the precision of his boundaries. He is not hostile -- he still points the player to the inn. But he makes it clear he is not an ally and the player is not entitled to his help.
