---date: 0005-01-01---The first year, I remember the buzz in the market, the vibrant new stalls. I saw a weaver from the north, her hands flying over the loom, creating patterns I'd never imagined. For a moment, watching her, I felt a deep ache and inspiration, thinking of all the unseen beauty in the world, and how much I wished my own songs could capture such intricate life. I hummed a new tune, feeling a fleeting sense of boundless possibility.---date: 0005-01-01---When the quiet illness settled over Hearthfield, the inn grew somber. I still played, but my songs became softer, more like lullabies than lively tunes. One evening, a young mother, pale and tired, sat weeping in a corner, her child fretful. Without thinking, I sang a gentle melody I’d written about quiet strength, and when she looked up, her eyes held a spark of gratitude that made my throat tighten. It felt like my music was finally serving a true purpose beyond just filling a room.---date: 0005-01-01---The lingering illness made me feel the town’s weariness like a stone in my own chest. I started spending more time by the river, just watching the water, trying to hum away the ache I felt for everyone. I overheard old farmer Silas grumbling about his dwindling crops and the fever in his household, and though I had little, I left a few of my meager coins on his porch later that night. It was a small gesture, but the weight of everyone’s struggles felt heavier than ever, even as I tried to lighten it for others.
