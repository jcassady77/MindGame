That first winter, the cold seemed to seep into everything, not just the stones of the inn, but into people's bones and hopes. I remember counting the sacks of flour, my heart a knot of worry, wondering how we'd all make it through. But then, I saw folks sharing their last bits, helping each other mend roofs, and even finding a quiet laugh over a meager meal. It was a stark reminder of how fragile life can be, but also how strong we are when we stand together. I watched Wren, too, carefully portioning out what little we had, learning the hard lessons of scarcity with a quiet grace that made my heart ache with pride and a fierce protectiveness.

The following harvest, it felt like the earth itself was breathing a sigh of relief, and so was I. The common room was overflowing again, not with worried whispers, but with the boisterous joy of full bellies and replenished stores. I recall seeing farmer Thomas, whose fields had been hit hardest, sharing a pint and a booming laugh with guild master Elara, a sight I thought I might never see again. It was more than just a good harvest; it was a testament to our resilience, a promise that Hearthfield would endure, and a deep gratitude for the simple abundance we had almost forgotten. Seeing the communal spirit reignited, I knew Marten would be smiling down, proud of our town and the future we were building for Wren.
