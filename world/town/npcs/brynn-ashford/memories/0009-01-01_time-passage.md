The completion of the new bridge over the Whisperwind River truly marked a turning point for Hearthfield, a physical manifestation of our growing strength. I remember standing on the common room steps, watching the last stone being set, feeling a deep swell of pride not just for the structure itself, but for what it represented: easier trade, closer families, and a future more secure for Wren. It's more than just wood and stone; it's a testament to what a community can achieve when it pulls together, and it's brought a new, steady flow of life through the inn's doors.

These past two years, the inn has rarely seen a quiet moment, and I wouldn't have it any other way. The common room hums with stories of successful harvests, new ventures, and families drawn to Hearthfield's growing reputation, a stark and welcome contrast to the lean years. I still find myself polishing the bar counter when I'm deep in thought, but now it's often with a quiet smile, listening to the contented chatter that fills the space I've worked so hard to build. It feels good to know this place is thriving, a true heart for the town.

Wren, bless her heart, has blossomed into such a capable young woman, her presence a quiet strength behind the counter and among the tables. She anticipates needs, remembers regulars' preferences, and even takes to the bookkeeping with a sharp eye I never quite had at her age. Watching her confidently navigate the bustling inn, a place I built with my own two hands and for her future, fills me with a deep, settled peace. Knowing she understands the rhythm of this life, and loves this town as much as I do, is the greatest reward.
