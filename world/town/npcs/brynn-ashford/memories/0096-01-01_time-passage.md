The earth's roar, the ground bucking beneath our feet – the earthquake was a terror I'd never known, leaving parts of our town shattered and folks white with fear. I remember the common room packed with people seeking shelter, their faces etched with shock, but just as quickly, the fear gave way to resolve. Seeing neighbors organize, sharing what little they had, and then the architects proposing those clever new techniques for rebuilding – it reaffirmed my absolute faith in Hearthfield's unyielding spirit and our capacity to mend, stronger than before, even against such an unexpected, primal threat.
