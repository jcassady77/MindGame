The new trade route brought a fresh buzz to Hearthfield, and the inn truly felt like the lively heart of it all. I remember the common room overflowing with new faces, merchants with tales from the north, and the clinking of coins that promised good seasons ahead. It was a joy to see the town swell with such robust life, knowing Marten would have seen this as a sign of true survival and growth for our home and for Wren's future.

The year of the lingering cough cast a different sort of quiet over Hearthfield, a heavy one that settled even in the usually boisterous common room. I spent many nights wiping down tables, listening to hushed voices, and brewing special teas for the ailing, my own energy often flagging under the weight of worry for my neighbors and, of course, for Wren. It was a stark reminder that even prosperity can't shield us from every hardship, and that true community means holding each other up when times are tough.

Through both the booming prosperity and the quiet illness, Wren has grown into such a thoughtful young woman, helping me manage the inn with a quiet efficiency that fills me with pride. She learned to mix remedies for the sniffles and became adept at comforting weary travelers, always with a kind word and a steady hand. Seeing her navigate these years, adapting and caring, strengthens my resolve; everything I do, every sacrifice, is for her bright, secure future here.
