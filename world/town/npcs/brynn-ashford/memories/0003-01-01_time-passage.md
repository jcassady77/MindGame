I remember last year's harvest feast, the common room packed wall-to-wall with laughter and good cheer. Seeing the inn thrive, not just as a business, but as the beating heart of Hearthfield, truly warmed my soul. Marten would have been proud to see how far we've come, how solid this place has become for Wren and for everyone else.

These last two years have settled into a comfortable hum, a steadiness I didn't know Hearthfield could achieve. There weren't any grand events, no big dramas, just the quiet turning of the seasons and the steady flow of familiar faces through my door. It’s a good kind of quiet, the kind that lets roots grow deep. It reminds me that sometimes, peace and predictable days are a blessing greater than any boom.
