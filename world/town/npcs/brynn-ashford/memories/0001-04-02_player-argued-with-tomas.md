I was clearing the tables outside when I heard the voices — the traveler and Tomas, right there in the square where anyone could see. Tomas is a gentle soul, the kind of man who apologizes to his own cabbages when he pulls them. And this stranger had him backed against the well wall, finger in his chest, voice hard enough to cut glass. I don't know what it was about and I don't much care. You don't talk to people like that in my town — not where Wren might be watching from the doorway. I liked this one after the window. Now I'm not so sure what I'm looking at.
