The common room was eerily quiet during the fever, a deep ache settling over Hearthfield. I remember seeing Elara Thornwick, her face etched with exhaustion, still mixing poultices and offering comfort to anyone who would listen, even as she herself looked on the verge of collapse. The strength of her spirit, even in the face of such overwhelming loss, was a beacon for us all, and I made sure she always had a warm meal and a quiet corner to rest in when she could. That time reminded me how vital our healers are, and how fragile life can be even in a thriving town.
