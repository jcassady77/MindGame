The wind took the front window shutter clean off its hinges last night, and I spent half the morning trying to hold the frame level while hammering it back on — which is exactly as useless as it sounds with two hands. The stranger walked up without a word, braced the frame with both hands, and held it steady while I drove the nails home. Didn't ask for a drink, didn't want coin. Just nodded and moved on. I've run this inn long enough to know that most people who do favors are buying something — but this one didn't seem to be shopping. I poured them a cider on the house that evening and made a point to remember their face.
