Gareth pulled me aside after supper and told me — quiet, the way he does when he's trying not to sound like a gossip — that the traveler may have helped themselves to stock from his shelves. He said he can't prove it, but the count was off after they left and nobody else came through that afternoon. Gareth is careful with his numbers, I'll give him that. But he's also been jumpy since the guild raised his dues, and jumpy men see thieves in every shadow. I don't want to believe it. That same person held my shutter frame in the rain. But I've learned the hard way that a kind hand and a light one can belong to the same person, so I'll keep my eyes open and my cashbox where I can see it.
