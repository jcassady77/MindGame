---date: 0007-01-01tags: [winter, loss, survival, cunning]---The bitter winter years ago was a drain on my ledger, fewer goods moving, fewer coins to weigh. It reinforced my conviction that true security comes not from communal spirit, but from the foresight to hoard and control essential resources. I recall the gnawing frustration of stalled caravans and empty market stalls.---
---date: 0007-01-01tags: [harvest, profit, greed, power]---The spring after the lean winter, the fields burst with life, a bounty ripe for the taking. I moved swiftly, securing contracts for the choicest grain and livestock, ensuring my warehouses were full before the town truly understood its fortune. It was a gratifying exercise in leveraging timing and influence.---
---date: 0007-01-01tags: [observation, control, town, weakness]---Watching the townspeople rally after the harsh winter, I noted their efforts, their 'communal spirit' as they called it. To me, it was simply a collective, necessary exertion to restore the conditions that allowed my enterprises to flourish. Their resilience, though admirable, only underscored my understanding that they would always rely on someone like me to guide their prosperity.
