The forge hummed with constant work for two years. I shaped many plowshares, fixed countless axes for the new families arriving in Hearthfield. Each strike of my hammer helped lay the town's roots a little deeper. Seeing the bountiful harvests always brought a quiet satisfaction.

Young Tomas, my apprentice, has grown steady at the anvil. I taught him the proper way to temper steel, just as my father taught me. He is a good boy, diligent and respectful, proving himself worthy of the craft. I judge a man by the hands he keeps, and Tomas has honest hands.

Three nights a week, I still go to Brynn's inn. We don't speak much, just a nod, a shared silence, a reliable mug of ale. Her presence is a quiet comfort, a steady anchor in my week's routine. Some things, like good iron, do not need many words to hold strong.
