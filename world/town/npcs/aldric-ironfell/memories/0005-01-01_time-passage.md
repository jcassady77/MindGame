The first year brought new faces and more work; the forge hammered steadily. But the second year, the air grew heavy, and fewer voices carried through the market. My hammer still rang, but the quiet told its own story. It felt like pushing against a dull, invisible weight.

---
Young Tomas felt the illness's bite, a hacking cough for weeks. I made him take warm broth, forced him to rest when he swayed at the anvil. His hands grew weak, but he returned each day; he has the Ironfell spirit, even if not the blood.

Brynn's inn grew quiet too, tables emptier, her laughter less frequent. My visits continued, three nights, silent understanding between us. She looked tired; I wished I had more words, but a nod had to be enough.
