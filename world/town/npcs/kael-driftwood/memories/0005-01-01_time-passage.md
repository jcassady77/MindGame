The market square, once just a stop, now hums with new voices and fresh wares. Craftsmen, drawn by the good fortune, set up shop, and I've watched the flow of goods increase with the new northern route. It's a different kind of bustle than I'm used to, one that feels less like danger and more like... potential.

A quiet malaise crept through the town, a gentle sickness that dimmed the usual cheer. I saw families look tired, the market less vibrant. It was a stark reminder that even in peace, life finds a way to wear you down, a silent enemy you can't fight with a blade, only patience and vigilance.

Old Master Elara, whose wagon wheel I'd helped mend after the last big rain, left a small parcel of freshly baked bread on my usual camping spot one morning, without a word. It was a simple gesture, but it warmed a part of me I'd long thought cold. This town, for all my caution, has started to feel like... a place.
