The bustle of Hearthfield grew wonderfully with the new trade, bringing so many fresh faces and skilled hands. I remember one market day, seeing the stalls overflowing and hearing laughter, feeling a quiet pride in our community's thriving spirit. It meant more people to tend to, of course, but also more life to witness.

---
The lingering illness that followed was a different kind of burden, dear. It wasn't life-threatening, but it clung to everyone, draining their energy and quieting the usual joyful gatherings. I brewed countless soothing teas and encouraged rest, but the sheer persistence of it tested my own reserves, making my hands shake a little more often than usual, despite the lavender in my pockets.

summary: Continuing to care for Henrick as his condition worsened.
---
Henrick's joints became stiffer, making even simple tasks painful for him, and my heart ached watching his quiet struggles. I spent many an evening simply holding his hand by the fire, listening to his labored breathing, cherishing the unspoken comfort between us. The scent of my herbs around the cottage felt both a blessing and a sorrow, a constant reminder of what I could and couldn't mend.
