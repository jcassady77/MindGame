The new bridge, dear, it truly brought a tear to my eye the day they opened it. I remember watching families from the outlying farms cross with such ease, their faces alight with connection, and thought of how much simpler it would make my own calls to those parts. It feels like a sturdy arm reaching out, holding our community closer, and that warms my heart more than any fire.

Henrick's hands, bless his stubborn soul, have become even more gnarled these past two years, making his daily tasks a silent battle. I spend longer now massaging his joints with warm oils, and sometimes he just sighs, a soft sound that echoes the ache in my own chest. Yet, he still tries to help with the kindling, and those small acts of resilience, his quiet presence, they are the anchors that steady me.
