---
My heart ached to see the joint sickness tighten its grip on Henrick's hands, making even a simple cup of tea a struggle for him. I spent more hours poring over my old books, searching for any forgotten remedy, though I knew I was truly just trying to outrun the inevitable. One evening, as I read him a story by the fire, he chuckled softly at a silly passage, and in that fleeting sound, I found a measure of peace.

Hearthfield's population expanded, bringing new challenges and new faces to my door, each needing a steady hand or a comforting word. I recall a particularly cold spring when several young families fell ill with a persistent cough, and I worked through the nights, brewing warm teas and chest rubs, feeling the weight of their well-being resting squarely on my shoulders. Yet, seeing their rosy cheeks return was its own profound reward, a quiet affirmation that my work here still mattered.
