summary: The grueling struggle through Hearthfield's harsh, protracted winter.
---
That first year, the cold seemed to seep into one's very bones and never leave, making every visit to the sick a slow, deliberate trudge through the biting winds. I remember the gnawing worry seeing the grain stores dwindle, and the quiet fear in people's eyes as I tried to reassure them, all while my own energy waned and Henrick shivered more often despite the extra blankets. My hands would shake even more, despite the lavender in my pockets, but I kept them steady enough for my poultices and teas.

date: 0007-01-01
summary: The profound relief and renewed spirit following the bountiful harvest.
---
The relief that washed over Hearthfield with the arrival of that spring and the subsequent bountiful harvest was almost palpable, like a great weight lifted from every shoulder. I remember watching children laugh and play in the fields again, their faces no longer pinched with winter's hunger, and feeling a quiet, deep warmth spread through my own heart. It was a testament to our community's resilience, a gentle reminder that even the deepest frosts eventually give way to vibrant life.
---
date: 0007-01-01
summary: Witnessing Henrick's quiet resilience and continuing his care.
---
Through the harsh winter and into the hopeful spring, Henrick's quiet strength continued to be a beacon for me, even as his joints ached with the perpetual cold. He never complained, just offered a tired smile, and I made sure his teas were always warm and his compresses fresh. One afternoon, I caught him humming along to my hymns as I dressed his hands, and in that simple moment, a profound sense of peace, hard-won and precious, settled over my weary soul.
