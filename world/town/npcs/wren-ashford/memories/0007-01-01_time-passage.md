During the bitter winter, I saw Mother share our meager stew with a freezing traveler, even when our own bellies ached. That night, I snuck out to the frozen river, not to climb, but to chip away at the ice for cleaner water, my fingers numb but my heart warm with a fierce need to help her. It wasn't the kind of adventure I usually sought, but it felt just as important, like I was truly part of keeping our family going.

When spring finally broke, the entire town worked side-by-side, planting and tilling, and I found myself laughing louder than I had in months. The first harvest was a blur of sun and sweat, but seeing the granaries fill again, it was like Hearthfield itself took a deep, happy breath. I climbed the tallest stack of grain sacks in the new granary, feeling the warmth of the sun and the hope spreading through everyone, a different kind of freedom than before, one tied to everyone else.

I still collect stones, but now I often find them near the fields where everyone worked, not just the river. I named a particularly smooth one 'Community' after seeing how we all pulled together. I also started asking Old Man Hemlock about the stars, not just about roads, because after the long winter, I wanted to understand how things moved in the sky, how the seasons turned, and what else was out there beyond our quiet, resilient town.
