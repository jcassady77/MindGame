The new clock tower in the market square was the hardest climb yet, but from the top, I could see all the new stalls and craftsmen that had arrived. Master Elara, the silversmith, looked up and waved when I peered down, and I felt a thrill seeing how much Hearthfield had grown. It was like the whole town was breathing deeper, filled with new stories to discover.

When the cough started going around, Mother seemed tired all the time, even though she always tried to smile. I saw the empty chairs in the common room more often, and the usual laughter was quieter. I tried to help more, bringing extra firewood or fetching water, and I’d sit by Mother's side, tracing the lines of her hand, wishing I could just climb up to the sky and make the sickness go away.

I found a perfectly smooth, grey stone near the northern road, right where the new wagons rumbled in, carrying goods from places I'd only dreamed of. I named it 'Journey' and tucked it into my pocket, imagining all the miles it had traveled to get here. The world still felt wide open, and even with the quiet illness, I knew there were always new horizons to explore, new questions to ask.
