I remember the day the new granary was finished. It looked so big and important, so of course, I had to climb it. From the top, Hearthfield looked like a toy town, all the little houses laid out like pebbles. I named the biggest roof beam 'Freedom' because that's exactly what I felt up there, wind in my hair, the whole world spread out below me. Mother would have skinned me alive if she knew, but it was worth it.

When the Miller family arrived, they looked lost, with so many boxes piled up outside their new home. Mother was busy, so I just started carrying their lightest bundles inside, even the funny ones wrapped in cloth. The littlest Miller girl, Elara, held onto my hand the whole time, and by the end of the day, we were drawing maps of the river in the dust, her shy smile making me feel a warmth I didn't expect.

I remember listening through the kitchen door one evening, Brynn talking with Old Man Thistle about the new trade route they hoped to open. He said it would 'knit Hearthfield into the wider world,' and Brynn nodded, looking thoughtful. I kept asking her what 'knit' meant for days afterward, trying to understand how a road could be like a needle and thread, and she just laughed and told me I'd find out soon enough.
