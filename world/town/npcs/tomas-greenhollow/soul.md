  made him laugh and because she wasn't afraid of the work. They have two boys

  talking instead of making. He pays his taxes, minds his fences, and asks
  nothing of the town except to be left alone to do what Greenhollows have
  always done. He is deeply suspicious of grand plans, heroic ambitions, and
  anyone who promises that things are about to get better, because in his
  experience, that usually means they're about to get worse for people like him.
