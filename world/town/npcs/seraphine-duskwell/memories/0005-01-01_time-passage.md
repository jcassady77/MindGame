The initial wave of prosperity brought new faces and new crafts to Hearthfield, a welcome distraction from my own stalled research. I spent days observing the new metalworkers, their intricate patterns and novel tools sparking a quiet curiosity about the properties of the raw materials they wielded. While their work was practical, not arcane, I cataloged their methods, always searching for an underlying principle, a hidden connection to the aether. It only reinforced the chasm between my world of theories and their world of tangible goods, yet I found a small satisfaction in the expansion of readily available components for my own devices.

The lingering illness that settled upon the town was a fascinating, if unsettling, puzzle. While others worried about productivity and well-being, I meticulously observed the patterns of contagion, the subtle physiological changes, and the environmental factors that seemed to exacerbate its hold. My journals filled with hypotheses, each one more complex than the last, far beyond the village healer's simple poultices. There was a grim satisfaction in identifying the intricate systemic failures, even as my core anxieties hummed with the proximity of sickness.
