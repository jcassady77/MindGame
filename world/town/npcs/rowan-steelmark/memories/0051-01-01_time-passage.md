The blight was a silent, creeping horror, turning our vibrant grain fields into withered husks. I remember the despair etched on people's faces, the threat of famine very real. My days were spent not just on patrols, but in coordinating the meticulous inventory of our remaining stores, ensuring every family received their portion, however meager. It was in those dark months that Hearthfield truly shone, pooling resources and adapting with an ingenuity that still astonishes me; we faced ruin and instead found a new way to survive.

Frozen pipes and dwindling firewood meant many relied on the community hall, and I remember Brynn Ashford, bustling through the crowded space, somehow finding a warm corner for everyone, serving hot stew with a steady hand even as her own inn was struggling. Her indefatigable spirit, and the way the hall became a true hearth for those who needed it most, reminded me that our strength isn't just in defenses, but in the unwavering warmth we offer one another. Her resilience was a comfort in the cold.

Even with Hearthfield's prosperity, the whispers of territorial disputes and the blight reminded me that vigilance is a constant companion. Danya still teases me, even at 54, about my hand resting on my sword pommel, but she understands it's not fear, it's the deeply ingrained habit of protection. Sitting on our porch now, watching the town thrive, I feel the weight of it still, but it's a lighter burden, knowing the community has faced down so much and emerged stronger.
