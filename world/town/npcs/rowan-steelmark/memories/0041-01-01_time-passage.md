The drought was a test of Hearthfield's spirit, the fields cracked and the river running low. I remember seeing Tomás Greenhollow's usually robust hands tremble as he inspected his parched crops, the fear of ruin stark in his eyes. My days were spent ensuring our strict water rationing was observed fairly, and it was the quiet, determined cooperation of everyone, from the newest settler to the oldest elder, that saw us through until the blessed rains arrived. That unity, born of shared hardship, felt like a renewed foundation for our community.

I remember Brynn Ashford, wading through knee-deep water outside her inn, her voice hoarse from shouting instructions, yet her resolve unbroken as she directed families to higher ground. The sheer force of the water was immense, but the town guard and citizens worked tirelessly, risking everything to rescue neighbors and salvage what little they could, a grim testament to our loyalty to one another.

These five years have shown me the full measure of Hearthfield's strength, from the shared anxiety of drought to the devastation of the flood, yet we always return to the quiet work of rebuilding. The establishment of the library and the expansion of apprenticeships, born from times of abundance, speaks to a deeper hunger for knowledge and growth, proving that our community understands the value of building for the future, not just surviving the present. Danya often says I carry the town's weight, and it's true, but it's a weight made lighter by the unwavering spirit of its people.
