The clang of the alarm bells on that fateful night still rings in my ears, the red glow from the western district painting the sky. When the origin was traced back to Aldric's forge, there was a murmur of anger in the town, but my priority was stopping the blaze and saving lives, not assigning blame. I remember speaking with Aldric amidst the smoldering ruins of the granary, his face etched with ash and despair; I told him we'd rebuild, together, and that his skill would be needed more than ever.

The years following the fire were arduous, marked by the smell of scorched earth and fresh-cut timber. My morning walks shifted from quiet patrols to overseeing reconstruction, seeing the community pull together brick by brick. I often stopped by the new dwellings being raised on the town's edge, remembering the families who had lost everything, and felt a renewed, heavy resolve to ensure Hearthfield stood stronger.

Through the bitter winter and the consuming fire, Danya was my steady anchor. There were evenings, after a particularly grueling day of coordinating supplies or mediating disputes, when I'd return home utterly drained. Her quiet presence, the way she'd simply listen without interruption, allowed me to carry the town's weight another day, knowing that even amidst the scars, Hearthfield's spirit, much like ours, remained unbroken.
