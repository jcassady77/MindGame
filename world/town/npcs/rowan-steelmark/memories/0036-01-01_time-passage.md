The fifth year brought the flood, an unexpected surge of the Whisperwind that twisted the market stalls and filled riverside cellars. I remember Brynn Ashford, her usual hearty laugh absent, standing grimly by her inn's waterlogged foundations, but even then, her first thought was for the families sheltered in the town hall. For weeks, my patrols shifted to coordinating relief and overseeing river defenses, seeing the weariness on every face, yet also the unwavering resolve of Hearthfield's people rebuilding, brick by water-stained brick.

A decade has passed, and I feel the years in my bones, yet the rhythm of Hearthfield remains my constant. Danya often remarks that my hand still rests on my sword pommel, even at 54, but now it's less about apprehension and more about the deeply ingrained habit of watchfulness. These quiet evenings on the porch, her hand in mine, watching the town lights twinkle, are a testament to the enduring loyalty and strength of the home we've protected, through both hardship and prosperity.
